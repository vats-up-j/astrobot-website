# Skill: Competitive Scan

Analyze top Indian astrology sites to identify gaps and opportunities for HoroTalk.

## When to trigger
- Strategy agent is planning the roadmap
- User asks "what are competitors doing?", "how do we differentiate?"
- Monthly strategy review

## Steps

1. **Identify competitors** — The key players in Indian online astrology:
   - **AstroSage** (astrosage.com) — largest Indian astrology portal
   - **Drik Panchang** (drikpanchang.com) — authoritative Panchang reference
   - **GaneshaSpeaks** (ganeshaspeaks.com) — paid consultation platform
   - **Kundli Software** (kundli.click, astrokundli.com) — free Kundli tools
   - **Aap&Astro** — WhatsApp-based competitor (if active)

2. **For each competitor, analyze** (use web search/fetch if available):
   - What pages/tools do they offer?
   - What's their primary traffic source? (SEO, social, ads)
   - What's their conversion model? (Ads, subscriptions, per-consultation)
   - What do they do well?
   - What do they do poorly? (UX, mobile, speed, trust)

3. **Gap analysis** — What do competitors have that HoroTalk doesn't?
   - Tools: Kundli generator, matching, transit charts, divisional charts
   - Content: Sign-specific pages, planet pages, nakshatra pages, festival calendars
   - Features: PDF reports, email subscriptions, push notifications
   - Trust: Years of operation, user reviews, media mentions

4. **Opportunity analysis** — Where can HoroTalk win?
   - Mobile experience (most competitors have cluttered desktop-first sites)
   - WhatsApp convenience (no one else does AI + WhatsApp well)
   - Clean modern design (vs. ad-heavy competitors)
   - Bilingual from day one
   - AI-powered personalization at scale

5. **Output format**:
   ```
   ## Competitive Scan — [date]

   ### Feature comparison matrix
   | Feature | AstroSage | Drik | Ganesh | HoroTalk |
   |---------|-----------|------|--------|----------|
   | [feature] | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N |

   ### Top opportunities (HoroTalk can win here)
   1. [opportunity] — Why: [reasoning] — Effort: [low/med/high]

   ### Threats (competitors are strong here)
   1. [threat] — Our response: [strategy]
   ```
